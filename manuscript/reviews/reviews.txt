Dear Mr. Swanepoel:

Decision on USYB-2024-141, TreeFlow: Probabilistic Modelling and Automatic Differentiation for Phylogenetics:

Minor Revisions.

Thank you for your Systematic Biology submission. It has been reviewed by Associate Editor Dr Claudia Solis-Lemus and three of the original reviewers from the prior submission. Their comments are listed at the end of this letter.

First, I would like to thank the authors for their hard work in addressing comments and suggestions throughout the previous review process. The original manuscript was difficult for the typical user to follow, and it lacked appropriate documentation for using the software. It also did not include a detailed comparison with other software utilizing a universal probabilistic programming language. The authors have taken two years to complete the revision, and I believe the end result represents a very valuable contribution. The new version includes a much better-documented online manual and a thorough comparison with other approaches.

Even though the revised manuscript is clearly on-track towards publication, this second round of review has identified some additional issues that need to be addressed before acceptance, i.e., improvements are needed in the description of the software, particularly the instructions for installation and running the analyses in the tutorial. Both reviewers #1 and #2 reported difficulties in running the software with the provided examples. Reviewer #3 has provided a detailed report with many useful suggestions that I am sure you will appreciate. See this reviewer's comments below on how to improve the figures and the description of the user interface, as well as the inclusion of additional references. This reviewer also requests that the empirical benchmarking section focuses on the advantages and new capabilities introduced by TreeFlow, rather than on the biological implications. I fully agree with this opinion.

Although these changes and some of the requests below may require more than minor revisions, I have designated the manuscript as "acceptance pending minor revisions" to encourage the authors to resubmit as soon as possible. We all agree that this is impressive work and that the manuscript deserves publication, but the suggested changes will enhance the usability of this software for the average Systematic Biology reader.

Finally, I would like to sincerely apologise for the extended review time for your manuscript, which exceeded our current turnover standards. I hope you find the final outcome satisfactory, making the wait worthwhile, and that the thorough review reports provided below are helpful in preparing your manuscript for final publication. Moving forward, I will ensure that the final resubmission undergoes a more expedited editorial review process.

Please acknowledge (by email to sysbio.editorialoffice@oup.com) receipt of the reviews and give a probable time frame for the return of your revised manuscript. Your revision should be submitted as soon as possible. Any revision not received in a timely manner may have to be considered a new submission.
